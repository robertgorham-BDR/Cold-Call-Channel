## Before Drafting: Classify the Contact

WARM or COOL determines everything. Get this right first.

| Tier | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| WARM-HOT | You spoke with them AND agreed on a specific next step |
| WARM | You spoke with them, no specific commitment |
| COOL | Same event, never connected directly |

If you're not sure — ask Rob. Do not guess.

---

## WARM Message Formula (proven at QonfX Bay and Irish Dreamin')

For contacts you actually spoke with. The goal is to continue a conversation, not start one.

### Structure (3 paragraphs, ~75-120 words)

**Para 1 — Reference the conversation**
Specific detail from your conversation at the event. What you talked about. What was memorable. Not generic "it was great meeting you."

**Para 2 — Bridge to Testsigma**
One sentence connecting what you discussed to what Testsigma solves. Keep it light — you've already done the pitch at the event. This is a follow-through, not a restart.

**Para 3 — CTA**
- If WARM-HOT (next step agreed): Reference the agreed next step explicitly. "You mentioned wanting to see the self-healing demo — happy to set that up."
- If WARM (no specific commitment): Soft ask — "Worth a quick 15 minutes to pick up where we left off?" or advance to a low-commitment next step.

### Hard Rules for WARM Messages

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| No generic opener | Never "It was great meeting you at [Event]" with nothing specific |
| No proof points | Avoid customer stories unless prospect specifically asked for evidence at the event |
| No meeting ask in T1 for WARM (unless HOT) | Light ask only — you're continuing a conversation, not opening cold |
| Em dashes | NONE — use commas |
| Subject | "Following up from [Event]" format works. No mystery needed — warm context IS the hook. |
| Sign-off | "Cheers," only |
| CC rule | If a colleague introduced the lead, CC them on T2 (not T1) |

## COOL Message Formula

COOL contacts (attended same event, never met) use the standard TAM T1 formula from `memory/sop-email.md` with one modification: **add a brief conference-context hook in Para 1.**

Conference-context opener examples:
- "We were both at QonfX last week — I didn't get a chance to connect in person but wanted to reach out."
- "I was at [Event] and noticed your name on the attendee list — figured I'd reach out directly."

After the hook, continue with the standard industry-truth Para 1 opener and normal T1 formula.

**Full COOL example (QonfX — Pankaj Bavishi, FinTech company):**
```
Subject: Pankaj's test coverage at [Company]

Hi Pankaj,

We were both at QonfX last week — I didn't get a chance to connect in person but wanted
to reach out. FinTech platforms that release frequently tend to accumulate test suite
brittleness fast, since payment validation and compliance logic shifts with each update.
Is framework stability a bigger drain than building new coverage right now?

CRED was dealing with the same coverage pressure and reached 90% regression coverage on
Testsigma using AI-generated tests written in plain English, 5x faster than before.

At your scale, that kind of gap compounds with every release. Is coverage debt something
your team is actively working to close?

Cheers,
```

**Do NOT use the WARM formula for COOL contacts.** It reads as false familiarity and will damage credibility. WARM contacts had a real conversation with Rob. COOL contacts did not.
