## Sequence Overview

### What Is This Sequence?

Conference Outreach is a **warm/cool follow-up motion** for prospects encountered at industry events. Key dynamics:

**WARM Contacts (Met in person or had substantive conversation):**
- Met at booth, had 10+ min conversation
- Exchanged business cards
- Discussed specific use case
- Attended your talk or session together
- **Reply Rate Expected:** 60–70% (existing relationship)
- **Meeting Rate:** 30–40% (pre-qualified, discussed value already)

**COOL Contacts (Event attendee, didn't meet personally):**
- Attended same conference
- Are in target persona + vertical
- Haven't been cold-outreached before
- **Reply Rate Expected:** 45–55% (event context makes it warmer than cold)
- **Meeting Rate:** 20–30% (event creates natural hook)

**Why Conference Outreach Converts Faster:**
- Shared event context = instant credibility ("I was at QAHub too")
- Either met in person (warm handoff) or clear ICP match (cool but relevant)
- Timing = they're thinking about these topics (at conference = active buyer)
- Lower friction than cold outreach ("remember seeing you at...")

### Event Cycle Timeline

```
8 WEEKS BEFORE:
- Identify event + register
- Build prospect list (who's speaking, who usually attends)
- Pre-event messaging (optional, "see you there?")

1–2 WEEKS BEFORE:
- Confirm attendance
- Prepare talking points, booth presence, demo

DURING EVENT (Days 1–3):
- Attend sessions, visit other booths
- Collect business cards / contact info from warm contacts
- Note conversations: name, company, specific discussion, contact info
- Log in event-specific tracker (real-time or at end of each day)

DAY 3–4 AFTER EVENT (T1 Warm Handoff):
- Send personal follow-up to WARM contacts ("was great meeting you at...")
- Reference specific conversation topic

DAY 5–7 AFTER EVENT (T1 Cool Outreach):
- Send event-context email to COOL contacts ("notice you attended QAHub...")
- Light personalization, higher engagement CTA

DAY 10–12 (T2 Follow-Up):
- For non-replies, send light T2 ("want to continue our conversation?")

DAY 21–30 (Analysis & Archival):
- Review event performance (reply rate, meeting rate)
- Archive tracker in analytics/dashboards/conference-[event-slug]-tracker.html
```

## Execution Flow

### Phase 1: During Event (Capture Contact Data)

**Real-Time Logging:**
1. Meet contact at booth or session
2. Capture: First name, last name, company, email, title, topic discussed, warmth level
3. Business card? Take photo (reference for later)
4. Add to event tracker (real-time in spreadsheet or notebook)

**Example Log Entry:**
```
Date: Apr 9, 2026, 2:30 PM
Name: Sarah Chen
Company: Acme Corp
Email: sarah@acme.com (on card)
Title: QA Manager
Context: Booth conversation, 15 min. Discussed flaky tests + maintenance cost. Interested in demo.
Warmth: WARM (engaged, took materials, said "would love to try it")
Next Step: T1 warm handoff email (Apr 10)
```

### Phase 2: T1 Warm Handoff (Day 1–2 After Event)

**Timing:** Send within 24–48 hours of event end (while memory fresh)

**Formula: Personal + Specific + Soft CTA**

```
Hi [First name],

Great connecting with you at QAHub yesterday—loved hearing about the test maintenance challenge at Acme.

One thing we talked about: the idea of "self-healing" tests that auto-update when UI changes. I'm going to send you a quick 5-min demo video that shows exactly how that works for your use case.

Let me know if you want to dig deeper. I'm also happy to hop on a brief call if that's easier.

Cheers,
[Signature]
```

**Key Elements:**
- Reference specific conversation ("the test maintenance challenge")
- Show you listened (not generic thank-you)
- Offer next step that's LOW friction (video link, not immediate meeting ask)
- Warmth level: HIGH (you met, you showed interest in their problems)

**CTA Options (Ranked by Conversion):**
1. "Sending you a demo video—let me know if you want to chat" (56% reply, highest)
2. "Would love to continue our conversation" (53% reply)
3. "What day works for a quick call?" (48% reply, still good but lower for warm)

**Email Length:** 70–90 words (shorter than cold outreach, relationship allows brevity)

**Send Within:** 24–48 hours (time-sensitive, memory is fresh)

### Phase 3: T1 Cool Outreach (Day 5–7 After Event)

**Timing:** Wait 3–5 days (not spam-like), gives Warm contacts time to reply first

**Formula: Event Context + Light Personalization + Engagement CTA**

```
Hi [First name],

Noticed you attended QAHub last week—great mix of people there.

I saw you're a QA Manager at Acme (noticed from the attendee list). We talked to several teams at the event about cutting test maintenance time in half using AI.

Might be relevant for your team. Would you be open to a quick conversation about what you're working on?

Cheers,
[Signature]
```

**Key Elements:**
- Reference shared event ("Noticed you attended QAHub")
- Light personalization from attendee list (title + company only, no deep LinkedIn research required)
- Value prop brief (maintenance time reduction)
- Soft ask ("would you be open to...")
- Warmth level: COOL (no personal meeting, but event context adds credibility)

**CTA Options (Ranked by Conversion):**
1. "What are you working on from a QA perspective?" (52% reply)
2. "Would love to hear what's on your roadmap" (50% reply)
3. "What day works for a quick call?" (45% reply, lower for cool)

**Email Length:** 75–90 words (similar to cold outreach, slightly warmer tone)

**Send Timing:** Day 5–7 after event (avoids spam look, gives time window for Warm replies first)

### Phase 4: T2 Follow-Up (Day 10–14 After Event)

**If no reply to T1:**

**For Warm Contacts:** More personal, assumption of interest
```
Hi [First name],

Quick check-in on that demo video I sent—was able to take a look?

No pressure if timing's not right. Just wanted to make sure you had everything you needed.

Cheers,
[Signature]
```

**For Cool Contacts:** Softer, don't assume interest
```
Hi [First name],

In case the timing is better now, sending a resource that might help with your evaluation.

[Link to case study or whitepaper]

Happy to chat if helpful.

Cheers,
[Signature]
```

**CTA:** Light engagement ask, not hard meeting push

**Email Length:** 50–70 words (short, brief follow-up)

## Warmth Classification System

### WARM Contacts (Priority 1)

**Criteria:**
- Had 10+ minute conversation with you or Rob
- Discussed specific use case or challenge
- Exchanged business cards
- Asked follow-up questions
- Showed engagement signals (leaned in, took materials, asked for demo)

**Response Time:** T1 within 24–48 hours (urgent)  
**Reply Rate Target:** 60–70%  
**CTA:** Soft (offer value, assume interest)  
**Meeting Rate:** 30–40% (pre-qualified)

### COOL Contacts (Priority 2)

**Criteria:**
- Attended same conference
- Are in target persona + vertical
- You didn't meet personally, but they show up on attendee list
- Haven't been cold-outreached before

**Response Time:** T1 on Day 5–7 (let warm contacts settle first)  
**Reply Rate Target:** 45–55%  
**CTA:** Medium (ask about their work, light soft ask)  
**Meeting Rate:** 20–30% (qualified by event + title + company)

### NO-FOLLOW Contacts (Priority 0, Skip)

- Already in MASTER_SENT_LIST.csv (cold-outreached before)
- On memory/do-not-contact.md
- On memory/domain-block-list.md
- Explicitly said "not interested" at event
- Company too small (< 50 employees) or not ICP-fit

## When Stuck: Decision Trees

### "Is this contact WARM or COOL?"

```
START: Reviewing event contact

DID you meet them in person at the event?
  → YES: Continue
  → NO: Jump to COOL classification

DID you have 10+ minute conversation?
  → YES: Continue
  → NO: COOL (event attendee, not personal meeting)

DID they ask follow-up questions or show engagement?
  → YES: WARM (engaged, showed interest)
  → NO: COOL (polite but not engaged)

CLASSIFICATION RESULT:
  → WARM: T1 within 24–48 hours
  → COOL: T1 on Day 5–7
```

### "What should my T1 email say?"

```
IS contact WARM (met in person, engaged conversation)?
  → YES: "Great connecting—here's the thing we discussed [specific]. Sending video/resource."
  → NO: Jump to COOL

IS contact COOL (event attendee, didn't meet personally)?
  → YES: "Noticed you at [event]—I saw you're in [role] at [company]. We work with teams like yours on [value]. Curious about your roadmap?"
  → NO: Unknown (ask Orchestrator)

BOTH:
- Reference shared event context (credibility)
- Personalization: specific for WARM, light for COOL
- CTA: Soft ask (not "What day works")
- 70–90 words
- End with "Cheers,"

RUN MQS GATE:
- For WARM: >= 8/12 (slightly lower bar, warmth makes up for polish)
- For COOL: >= 8/12 (similar bar to warm)
```

## Common Friction & Edge Cases

### Friction: Contact Already Cold-Outreached

**Situation:** Cool contact exists in MASTER_SENT_LIST.csv from Factor-First batch (already sent T1)

**Decision Logic:**
1. Check MASTER_SENT_LIST.csv → did they reply to original T1?
   - If replied (warm): use warm reply protocol (reply-router skill) instead
   - If no reply yet (in T2 window): don't send overlapping email, wait for T2 decision
   - If no reply + past T2 window: can send event follow-up ("saw you at event, want to revisit?")

**Best Practice:** Before sending T1 from event tracker, cross-check MASTER_SENT_LIST.csv

### Friction: Email Bounce After Event

**Situation:** T1 bounces with "invalid email" or "address not found"

**Possible Causes:**
1. Business card has old email (contact changed companies)
2. Contact gave wrong email at event (transcription error)
3. Email domain typo in card

**Fix:**
1. Ask Rob (if he has contact info from event)
2. Search LinkedIn (work Chrome) for updated email
3. Check company website (for updated directory)
4. If all else fails: skip contact, move on

### Friction: Same Contact at Multiple Events

**Situation:** Sarah Chen from Acme shows up at QAHub Q1 (Apr 10) and TestAutomation Days (Jun 5)

**Decision Logic:**
1. First event (Apr 10): Send T1 warm handoff
2. Second event (Jun 5): Don't send duplicate T1, acknowledge continuity
   - "Funny, we keep running into each other at conferences—must mean something!"
   - Continue T2 conversation instead of resetting
